Doug Williams, PE

As a Principal of ICON, Doug has a wide variety of engineering experience in water resources planning, design and construction. He has completed numerous drainage and flood control projects ranging from less than $10,000 to over $3M in construction costs. His practical approach to problem solving has been coupled with an awareness of aesthetics that has produced facilities that are functional as well as community amenities. Doug has successfully completed projects for the Urban Drainage Flood Control District (UDFCD) and local cities, counties, and towns throughout the Rocky Mountain Region.

Matt Ursetta, PE

As a Principal at ICON, Matt has 33 years of experience in general civil engineering, including water resources, park development and design, roadway design, utility design, and development services. His water resource experience includes hydrologic and hydraulic analysis, water distribution system analysis, preparation of feasibility studies, and preliminary and final design. Matt’s experience also includes projects related to trails, bridges, floodplains, hydraulics structures, and transportation/roadways.

Craig Jacobson, PE, CFM

As a Principal at ICON, Craig has a strong background in water resource engineering with an emphasis in floodplain management and hydrologic and hydraulic analyses. Craig has managed and designed a large array of stormwater improvements throughout the state of Colorado. Many of these projects have included conduit and pipe systems, channel stabilization and restoration, maintenance, detention, and water quality. Along with his design experience, Craig’s projects routinely involve the preparation of specifications and project documents, cost estimating, quantity take-offs, and construction management. He is a Certified Floodplain Manager (CFM) and instructs classes on the ASFPM certification through CASFM. Through a contract with the Urban Drainage and Flood Control District (UDFCD) funded by FEMA, he is responsible for technical reviews of FEMA Letters of Map Changes within the Denver Metro region. His project experience has included steady state and two-dimensional hydraulic modeling, stormwater improvement and channel stabilization designs, channel restoration and maintenance, master planning, and construction management. Additionally, he has completed benefit-cost analyses for use in FEMA PDM grant applications. He has extensive knowledge in the use of HEC-RAS, HEC-1; HEC-2; Flo-2D; SWMM 5.0, UD-SWMM CUHP, AutoCAD and GIS based systems.

Troy Carmann, PE, CFM

Troy is a Principal of ICON specializing in GIS and stormwater control projects along the Front Range. His design work includes stream restoration, channel stabilization, and flood control projects across numerous tributaries in the South Platte River Basin. Project designs have required coordination with the Army Corps of Engineers, Environmental Consultants, US Fish and Wildlife Service, EPA, CDPHE, and local municipal entities. Additionally, Troy served as Resident Engineer on a $3M stormwater project in the City of Fort Collins, a $2 million City of Colorado Springs channel stabilization project, and several local stream restoration projects. He directs ICON’s GIS Department and has been responsible for the production of countywide DFIRMs through our FEMA Map Modernization projects in Colorado, South Dakota and Wyoming. CWCB work includes the Benefit Cost Analysis for the State Flood Rules. Troy routinely presents this work to city councils, commissions, and boards and has made multiple presentations at national, state, and local conferences.
