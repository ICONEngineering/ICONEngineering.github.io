Otero Tributary

Stream Restoration

The Southeast Metro Stormwater Authority (SEMSWA) is planning channel improvements on the Otero Tributary within The Highlands 460 Neighborhood. Construction of these improvements is anticipated to take place between November 2021 â€“ May 2022, and is designed to make the stream corridor safer and more resilient moving forward. Learn more about the project below.

Project Background

Beginning at Phillips Avenue and extending 900-feet downstream, Otero Tributary is highly degraded. Tall and steep channel banks pose a safety hazard to pedestrians using the corridor. The proposed project will stabilize the grade of the channel using a series of small boulder drop structures. Channel banks will be graded to create safer access to the creek. The open space areas disturbed during the construction process will be restored with native plants and grasses. The project will also replace the damaged stormwater sewer system on Phillips Avenue. New stormwater inlets will be installed on Phillips Avenue with new storm sewer piping to safely convey stormwater into the creek. Damaged sections of sidewalk, curb, and roadway near the inlets will be replaced in conjunction with the project.
